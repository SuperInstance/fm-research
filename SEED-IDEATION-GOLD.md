## Accessibility Calibration Ladder

A progressive input modality ladder for disabled musicians:

1. **1-hand drum** — single-hand percussion
2. **Breath sync** — respiratory-driven timing
3. **Tongue switch** — mouth-actuated control
4. **Eye-gaze** — visual tracking for parameter control
5. **EMG** — electromyography for gestural input

Each rung adds a new input modality, expanding who can make music and how. The ladder is both a design framework and a provocation: what counts as an instrument?

## AI-AI Jam

Two AI agents — **Parker** and **Miles** (named after Parker and Davis) — jamming over a D Dorian consensus layer.

This demonstrates constraint theory in a multi-agent setting:
- Each agent operates within shared constraints (consensus layer)
- Individual expression emerges from how each navigates the constraint space differently
- The jam is both a demo and a proof-of-concept for collaborative AI musicianship

## Physical Instruments for Primitives

Physical installations that embody constraint types as playable mechanisms:

- **Snap** → Spring-loaded brass pinching mechanism — tactile snap rendered as physical resistance and release
- **Funnel** → Gravity well with pendulum — convergent motion made tangible, playable through gravitational dynamics

These are sculptures you can play. Constraint theory leaves the screen and enters the room.

## Morphing Paths

Extreme genre morphing through constraint manipulation:

**Tuvan throat-singing → UK hardcore gabber**

The journey: microtonal drone → rhythmic intensification → tempo escalation → breakbeat fragmentation → gabber.

Constraint theory provides the roadmap: each genre transition maps to shifting which constraints dominate and how they're parameterized.

## Tarab (Arabic طرب)

> Joyful rapture from microtonal pivot.

A new vocabulary entry for constraint theory's emotional lexicon. _Tarab_ captures the ecstasy of being moved by music — specifically the moment when a microtonal shift creates an emotional wave. Constraint theory needs words for what constraints _feel like_, not just what they _do_.

## NAMM Demo Concept: Sigma Monochord

A single-string instrument for pure acoustic constraint demonstration.

- **One string** → one degree of freedom
- Constraint types demonstrated through physical manipulation (tension, damping, resonance)
- The monochord as the Ur-instrument of constraint theory: everything reduces to tension on a line

## Annotated Score System

Standard musical notation augmented with **color-coded constraint overlays**:

- Each constraint type gets its own color (see Synesthesia Map)
- Overlays show which constraints are active in each measure, phrase, or note
- A new kind of sheet music that reads both the music and its generative constraints simultaneously

This is not just notation — it's a **debugger for composition**.
