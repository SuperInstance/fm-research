# Exercise Catalog

> Complete index of all exercises generated by `constraint-theory-core`'s exercise generator.
> 4 topics × 3 difficulties = 12 exercise types, each with multiple templates.

---

## How to Use This Catalog

Each exercise can be generated programmatically:

```python
from constraint_theory_core.exercises import generate_exercise

ex = generate_exercise("species_counterpoint", "beginner", seed=42)
print(ex["instructions"])
```

Or via CLI:

```bash
python -m constraint_theory_core exercise --topic species_counterpoint --difficulty beginner --seed 42
```

Use `--show-solution` to reveal the answer key and `--json` for machine-readable output.

## Topic 1: Species Counterpoint

Classical counterpoint exercises grounded in Eisenstein A₂ lattice theory, Laman rigidity, and holonomy verification.

### Beginner

- **Learning Objective:** Write consonant first-species counterpoint using interval constraints mapped to the A₂ lattice.
- **Estimated Time:** 15–20 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Blank staff paper; piano or notation software for checking intervals
- **Constraint Types Covered:** SNAP (consonant intervals only), FUNNEL (begin/end on perfect consonance), A₂ lattice snap safety
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **First-species above cantus firmus** — Compose an upper counterpoint to a 7-note cantus firmus in C major. All intervals must be consonant and A₂-safe. No parallel perfect fifths or octaves. *(Rubric: consonance 25, lattice_snap 25, no_parallels 25, start_end 25)*
  2. **First-species below cantus firmus** — Compose a lower counterpoint under a 7-note cantus firmus starting on G. Same consonance and lattice requirements; final interval must be octave or unison. *(Rubric: consonance 25, lattice_snap 25, no_parallels 25, resolution 25)*
  3. **Interval classification on the A₂ lattice** — Given a reference pitch and six test pitches, compute snap distances, classify intervals as safe/unsafe, and report covering-radius errors. *(Rubric: snap_accuracy 30, safety_classification 30, error_report 20, explanation 20)*

### Intermediate

- **Learning Objective:** Compose second-, third-, and fourth-species counterpoint with dissonance handling and Laman constraint verification.
- **Estimated Time:** 25–35 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Staff paper; access to `constraint-theory-core` for holonomy/Laman checks
- **Constraint Types Covered:** SNAP (strong-beat consonance), TEMPO (2:1, 4:1, syncopated rhythms), holonomy cycle verification, Laman counting (2n−3)
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Second-species (2:1)** — Compose counterpoint with consonant strong beats and passing-tone weak beats. Verify holonomy around every 3-interval cycle. *(Rubric: strong_beat_consonance 25, weak_beat_tolerance 20, holonomy 30, no_parallels 25)*
  2. **Third-species (4:1)** — Four-against-one with at least 2 consonant notes per group of 4. Verify Laman constraint count (2n−3). *(Rubric: consonant_density 25, laman_constraint 30, lattice_snap 20, cadence 25)*
  3. **Fourth-species (suspensions)** — Syncopated counterpoint with suspension chains resolving down by step. Model each suspension-resolution as a Laman edge. *(Rubric: suspension_dissonance 25, resolution_consonance 25, lattice_snap 25, variety 25)*

### Advanced

- **Learning Objective:** Compose multi-voice counterpoint with global holonomic consistency and Laman-rigidity verification.
- **Estimated Time:** 45–60 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Notation software; `constraint-theory-core` with full verification pipeline
- **Constraint Types Covered:** All previous + multi-voice Laman graphs, holonomy on fundamental cycles, invertible counterpoint, canonic technique
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Five-voice motet fragment (8 measures)** — Full SATBB counterpoint with Laman-rigid voice-pair graph, global holonomy verification, and cadential suspension. *(Rubric: pairwise_consonance 20, holonomy 25, laman_rigidity 25, no_parallels 15, cadence 15)*
  2. **Invertible counterpoint at the octave** — Three-voice counterpoint that remains consonant under voice permutation, with holonomy preserved. *(Rubric: invertibility 25, consonance_original 20, consonance_inverted 20, holonomy 20, laman 15)*
  3. **Double canon (4 voices, 6 measures)** — Two subjects at different temporal offsets with Laman-rigid constraint network and holonomy on all fundamental cycles. *(Rubric: canonic_accuracy 25, consonance 20, laman 20, holonomy 20, snap_report 15)*

## Topic 2: Voice Leading

Smooth voice-leading exercises connecting chords via minimal semitone movement on the A₂ lattice.

### Beginner

- **Learning Objective:** Connect triads with minimal voice movement; verify each voice snaps safely on the A₂ lattice.
- **Estimated Time:** 10–15 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Staff paper; chord spelling reference
- **Constraint Types Covered:** FUNNEL (smoothest path between chords), SNAP (A₂-safe movements), voice-crossing prohibition
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **C major → F major** — Smooth SATB transition; each voice moves ≤ 3 semitones, all A₂-safe, no crossings. *(Rubric: minimal_movement 30, no_crossings 20, lattice_snap 25, correct_target 25)*
  2. **Four-chord sequence (C→Am→F→G)** — Optimize total voice-leading distance across four transitions. *(Rubric: minimal_distance 30, no_parallels 20, a2_safe 25, voice_crossings 25)*
  3. **Triad distance matrix** — Compute A₂ voice-leading distances between all pairs of 7 major triads; identify minimum-distance pair. *(Rubric: distance_computation 35, matrix_completeness 25, min_identification 25, a2_usage 15)*

### Intermediate

- **Learning Objective:** Realize Bach-style chord progressions with Laman-rigid voice-leading graphs; explore neo-Riemannian transformations.
- **Estimated Time:** 25–35 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Staff paper; `constraint-theory-core` for Laman/holonomy checks
- **Constraint Types Covered:** FUNNEL (global optimization), Laman rigidity on 4-voice graphs, PLR transformations, holonomy around transformation chains
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Bach chorale realization (8 chords)** — Given soprano and bass, fill alto/tenor with Laman-rigid voice-leading constraints (2n−3 = 5 edges). *(Rubric: voice_leading_smoothness 25, laman_rigidity 25, holonomy 25, style 25)*
  2. **Neo-Riemannian PLR chain** — Connect C major to C♯ major in ≤ 6 PLR moves, each shifting one voice by semitone. *(Rubric: plr_correctness 30, minimality 25, a2_snap 25, holonomy 20)*
  3. **12-tone row voice leading** — Compute A₂ distance between a tone row and its retrograde-inversion; verify Laman rigidity on 12 pitch-class nodes. *(Rubric: ri_correctness 25, distance_computation 25, laman_check 25, max_displacement 25)*

### Advanced

- **Learning Objective:** Design globally optimized voice-leading networks; model voice leading as temporal funnels with Laman-rigid constraint graphs.
- **Estimated Time:** 45–60 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Notation software; `constraint-theory-core` full pipeline
- **Constraint Types Covered:** Full PLR network, algebraic connectivity, temporal funnel convergence, combined Laman + holonomy + funnel
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **24-triad PLR network** — Build complete voice-leading graph for all major/minor triads with A₂-weighted edges; verify local Laman rigidity. *(Rubric: graph_construction 25, local_laman 25, algebraic_connectivity 25, analysis 25)*
  2. **Globally optimized homophonic passage (12 chords)** — 4-voice passage with temporal funnel convergence and Laman-rigid inter-voice constraints. *(Rubric: global_optimality 25, funnel_convergence 25, laman_rigidity 25, holonomy 25)*
  3. **Mystic chord → French +6 voice leading** — Minimal A₂-distance path from Scriabin's mystic chord to French augmented sixth. *(Rubric: pitch_class_mapping 20, minimal_vl 30, laman_combined 25, holonomy 25)*

## Topic 3: Harmonic Constraints

Chord-progression exercises modeling harmony as constraint satisfaction on the A₂ lattice.

### Beginner

- **Learning Objective:** Identify harmonic constraints in common chord progressions; build basic constraint graphs.
- **Estimated Time:** 10–15 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Chord spelling reference; paper for graph drawing
- **Constraint Types Covered:** SNAP (shared pitch classes between adjacent chords), FUNNEL (common-tone preservation), A₂ lattice mapping of shared tones
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Progression classification** — Given 4 chord progressions, identify which satisfy the common-tone constraint (adjacent chords share ≥ 1 pitch class). *(Rubric: classification 30, a2_verification 25, gap_analysis 25, explanation 20)*
  2. **Diatonic triad graph** — Build a 7-node graph (I–vii°) with edges for shared pitch classes; verify connectivity and compute degrees. *(Rubric: graph_construction 30, connectivity 20, a2_mapping 25, degree_report 25)*
  3. **Smooth/rough transition analysis** — For C→G→Am→F→C, compute A₂ distance at each transition and classify as smooth (≤ 3) or rough (> 3). *(Rubric: distance_computation 30, classification 25, common_tones 20, smoothest_id 25)*

### Intermediate

- **Learning Objective:** Construct Laman-rigid harmonic constraint graphs; analyze modulation as cross-regional holonomy.
- **Estimated Time:** 25–35 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Staff paper; `constraint-theory-core` for Henneberg construction and holonomy checks
- **Constraint Types Covered:** Laman rigidity via Henneberg construction, holonomy across tonal regions, pivot-chord analysis, algebraic connectivity
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Laman-rigid 16-bar progression** — Select 6–8 chords, build Laman graph (2n−3 edges) via Henneberg construction, verify smooth transitions. *(Rubric: progression_quality 20, laman_construction 30, edge_validity 25, connectivity 25)*
  2. **Modulation analysis (C → G → C)** — Identify pivot chords, build sub-graphs per tonal region, verify holonomy within and across regions. *(Rubric: pivot_identification 25, subgraph_construction 25, holonomy_per_region 25, boundary 25)*
  3. **Chord-quality lattice** — Build a lattice of 5 chord qualities connected by single-semitone shifts; verify local Laman rigidity; identify most central quality. *(Rubric: lattice_construction 25, a2_distances 25, laman_verification 25, centrality 25)*

### Advanced

- **Learning Objective:** Design generative harmonic grammars as Laman-rigid CSP systems; model cross-tonal harmony.
- **Estimated Time:** 45–60 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** `constraint-theory-core` full pipeline; notation software
- **Constraint Types Covered:** Generative grammars, CSP formulation, cross-tonal Laman + holonomy, Bach analysis
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Generative harmonic grammar** — Design a Laman-rigid grammar that produces only valid progressions with A₂ distance ≤ 2 and holonomic consistency. *(Rubric: grammar_validity 25, laman_rigidity 25, smoothness_guarantee 25, holonomy 25)*
  2. **Bach chorale CSP analysis** — Model a chorale phrase as a constraint satisfaction problem; solve and compare with Bach's original. *(Rubric: csp_formulation 25, laman_rigidity 25, solution_quality 25, bach_comparison 25)*
  3. **Cross-tonal constraint system (C major / A minor)** — Build independent Laman graphs for two related keys with holonomy-consistent pivot chords. *(Rubric: regional_laman 25, pivot_consistency 25, cross_holonomy 25, completeness 25)*

## Topic 4: Rhythmic Constraints

Rhythmic composition exercises using temporal constraints, metronome alignment, and funnel convergence.

### Beginner

- **Learning Objective:** Construct simple rhythmic patterns aligned to metronome ticks; introduce temporal funnels.
- **Estimated Time:** 10–15 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Paper for rhythm notation; metronome (app or physical)
- **Constraint Types Covered:** TEMPO (restricted rhythm values), metronome tick alignment, temporal funnel (tolerance tightening)
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **4-measure pattern (quarters and halves)** — 4/4 time, ♩=120, every onset on a metronome tick, at least one half note per measure. *(Rubric: note_values 25, metronome_alignment 30, measure_completeness 20, variety 25)*
  2. **Two-voice rhythmic duet (3/4)** — Voice 1: quarter notes; Voice 2: dotted half notes. Verify subset alignment at ♩=100. *(Rubric: voice_1_correctness 25, voice_2_correctness 25, subset_verification 25, metronome_check 25)*
  3. **8-beat pattern with temporal funnel** — Eighths and quarters summing to 8 beats; tolerance decreases from ±0.1 to 0.0 over the exercise. *(Rubric: duration_accuracy 25, metronome_alignment 25, funnel_convergence 25, note_values 25)*

### Intermediate

- **Learning Objective:** Handle polymeter, syncopation, and hocket using temporal funnels and Laman anti-coincidence constraints.
- **Estimated Time:** 25–35 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** Metronome; `constraint-theory-core` for Metronome class and funnel verification
- **Constraint Types Covered:** Polymetric LCM alignment, syncopation displacement, hocket (anti-coincidence), Laman anti-coincidence edges, deadband funnels
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **Polymetric exercise (4/4 vs 3/4)** — Two voices at ♩=90; find LCM alignment (12 beats); verify funnel convergence to LOCKED phase. *(Rubric: lcm_correctness 25, metronome_sync 25, funnel_convergence 25, pattern_quality 25)*
  2. **Syncopated 8-bar rhythm** — All strong beats displaced by +1 eighth note; deadband funnel of ±0.25 tightening to LOCKED by bar 6. *(Rubric: displacement_accuracy 25, funnel_phases 25, deadband_compliance 25, bar_completeness 25)*
  3. **3-voice hocket** — Non-overlapping subdivisions across 12 beats; Laman anti-coincidence constraint (3 edges for 3 voices). *(Rubric: no_overlap 30, complete_coverage 25, metronome_sync 20, laman_constraint 25)*

### Advanced

- **Learning Objective:** Design distributed metronome consensus systems; construct Euclidean rhythms; build multi-layer temporal constraint systems.
- **Estimated Time:** 45–60 minutes per exercise
- **Required Materials:** `constraint-theory-core` full pipeline; DAW or notation software for playback
- **Constraint Types Covered:** Distributed consensus, Laman coupling graphs, Euclidean rhythms (Björklund's algorithm), multi-layer tempo consensus, algebraic connectivity
- **Templates (3):**
  1. **5-piece ensemble metronome consensus** — 5 independent metronomes with ±0.5 BPM drift; Laman coupling (7 edges); converge to LOCKED within 8 cycles. *(Rubric: consensus_convergence 25, laman_coupling 25, funnel_phases 25, algebraic_connectivity 25)*
  2. **Euclidean rhythm E(5,8)** — Generate Björklund pattern; verify maximal evenness via A₂ distance; temporal funnel convergence over one cycle. *(Rubric: euclidean_correctness 30, evenness_verification 25, funnel_convergence 25, a2_distance 20)*
  3. **Multi-layer rhythmic system (4 tempi)** — Independent layers at ♩=60, 80, 100, 120; LCM alignment; Laman-rigid cross-layer consensus (5 edges); holonomy on cross-layer cycles. *(Rubric: lcm_computation 20, laman_rigidity 20, holonomy 20, funnel_convergence 20, cross_layer_consensus 20)*
