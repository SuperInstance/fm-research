# Assessment Rubrics: Constraint-Based Compositions

> Detailed grading rubrics for evaluating student compositions at beginner, intermediate, and advanced levels.

---

## How to Use These Rubrics

Each level has a total of **100 points** divided across criteria. The criteria shift in emphasis as students advance:

| Level | Primary Focus | Weight Distribution |
|-------|--------------|-------------------|
| Beginner | Did they follow the constraints? | Compliance-heavy |
| Intermediate | Is it musically effective? | Balanced compliance + quality |
| Advanced | Do they understand *why* it works? | Analytical + creative |

**Grading philosophy:** These rubrics reward both rigor (following constraints) and creativity (making good music within them). A student who follows all constraints but produces dull music should score around 70–75%. A student who breaks constraints slightly but creates something compelling should score similarly. The highest scores require *both*.

---

## Beginner Level Rubric

**For:** Students in their first 1–2 sessions with constraint theory.
**Typical assignment:** 3–5 pitch composition with 1–2 rhythm values, 4–8 bars.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Points | Excellent (Full) | Proficient (¾) | Developing (½) | Beginning (¼) |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **SNAP Compliance** | 25 | Used only allowed pitches; no violations | 1–2 accidental violations | 3–4 violations | Systematic disregard for pitch constraint |
| 2 | **FUNNEL Compliance** | 20 | Starts and/or ends on required note(s) | Ends close to target (1 step away) | Ends on wrong note but in correct scale | No evidence of funnel awareness |
| 3 | **TEMPO Compliance** | 20 | Used only allowed rhythm values | 1–2 rhythm violations | Mixed correct/incorrect rhythms | Ignored rhythm constraint entirely |
| 4 | **Musical Coherence** | 20 | Sounds like a melody, not random; has shape | Mostly coherent with 1–2 awkward moments | Rambling but recognizable as music | Random-sounding with no shape |
| 5 | **Completeness** | 15 | Correct length (fills all bars); no missing beats | Mostly complete, 1 beat short/long | Partially complete | Incomplete or vastly wrong length |

### Beginner Scoring Guide

| Score | Grade | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 90–100 | A | Fully compliant, musically engaging. Ready for intermediate work. |
| 80–89 | B | Strong compliance, decent musicality. Minor issues. |
| 70–79 | C | Follows most constraints; musicality developing. On track. |
| 60–69 | D | Partial compliance; struggles with coherence. Needs support. |
| Below 60 | F | Significant non-compliance or incomplete work. |

### Beginner Feedback Templates

**For high-scoring students (90+):**
> "Excellent constraint discipline. Your melody shows that you understand how limits create focus. To level up: try varying your contour — right now your melody [mostly goes up / stays flat / repeats a pattern]. Can you create more shape while staying within the constraints?"

**For middle-scoring students (70–89):**
> "Good work following the rules! The constraint compliance is [strong / developing]. For next time, focus on [specific criterion]. Here's a tip: [concrete suggestion]."

**For lower-scoring students (below 70):**
> "I can see you engaging with the concept. Let's simplify: try the same exercise with [fewer pitches / simpler rhythms]. The goal isn't perfection — it's hearing how constraints shape your choices. Let's work together on [specific issue]."

## Intermediate Level Rubric

**For:** Students who have completed 2–3 sessions and demonstrate comfort with basic constraints.
**Typical assignment:** Multi-constraint compositions, 8–16 bars, pentatonic or diatonic pitch sets.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Points | Excellent (Full) | Proficient (¾) | Developing (½) | Beginning (¼) |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Constraint Compliance** | 20 | All constraints followed precisely | Minor violations (1–2 notes/beats) | Partial compliance | Systematic non-compliance |
| 2 | **Musical Shape** | 20 | Clear arc (tension → climax → resolution); contour is purposeful | Some shape but could be stronger | Flat or random contour | No discernible shape |
| 3 | **Constraint Creativity** | 20 | Uses constraints as *creative material* (not just following rules); surprising moments within the limits | Some creative constraint use | Follows rules mechanically | Doesn't engage creatively |
| 4 | **Rhythmic Interest** | 15 | Varied rhythm within the constraint; groove and momentum | Some variety | Repetitive rhythm | Monotonous |
| 5 | **Tonal Direction** | 15 | Clear sense of home (tonic); melody feels like it "goes somewhere and comes back" | Mostly directed | Wanders | No tonal center |
| 6 | **Polish** | 10 | Clean notation (or clear performance); dynamics or articulation present | Mostly clean | Sloppy but legible | Unclear/illegible |

### Intermediate Scoring Guide

| Score | Grade | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 90–100 | A | Constraints are a creative engine, not a cage. Musical and inventive. |
| 80–89 | B | Strong musicality within constraints. Minor shape or variety issues. |
| 70–79 | C | Compliant and musical but not yet creative with constraints. Safe. |
| 60–69 | D | Compliance or musicality inconsistent. Needs more exploration. |
| Below 60 | F | Doesn't demonstrate intermediate-level understanding. |

### Intermediate Feedback Templates

**For high-scoring students (90+):**
> "You've moved beyond 'following rules' into 'using rules.' Your composition shows real understanding of how constraints create identity. [Specific moment] was particularly effective because [reason]. For the advanced level, try [extension suggestion]."

**For middle-scoring students (70–89):**
> "Solid work. Your compliance is [strong], and the musicality is [developing/good]. To push toward the next level, ask yourself: 'What would this sound like if I leaned *into* the constraint instead of just staying within it?' Try [specific experiment]."

## Advanced Level Rubric

**For:** Students who have completed the full curriculum and demonstrate both technical skill and conceptual understanding.
**Typical assignment:** Multi-movement or multi-constraint compositions with analytical component, 16+ bars.

### Criteria

| # | Criterion | Points | Excellent (Full) | Proficient (¾) | Developing (½) | Beginning (¼) |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------------|-----------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 1 | **Constraint Mastery** | 15 | Flawless compliance; constraints are invisible in performance | Minor issues | Noticeable violations | Fundamental misunderstandings |
| 2 | **Compositional Craft** | 20 | Professional-quality writing; phrasing, pacing, and form are excellent | Good craft with minor issues | Adequate but unremarkable | Weak fundamental skills |
| 3 | **Constraint-Art Integration** | 20 | The composition *sounds like* the constraints. Removing any constraint would diminish the piece. | Constraints clearly shape the music | Constraints present but replaceable | No relationship between constraints and musical result |
| 4 | **Analytical Writing** | 15 | Articulates clearly *why* and *how* constraints shaped compositional decisions; specific examples | General understanding with some specifics | Vague or generic analysis | No meaningful reflection |
| 5 | **Structural Thinking** | 15 | Demonstrates understanding of how constraints interact (SNAP + FUNNEL + TEMPO as a *system*, not isolated rules) | Some systemic understanding | Treats constraints independently | No systemic awareness |
| 6 | **Originality** | 15 | Distinctive voice; the composition is recognizably *theirs* | Some personality | Generic but competent | Derivative |

### Advanced Scoring Guide

| Score | Grade | Interpretation |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 90–100 | A | Publishable-quality work with deep conceptual understanding. |
| 80–89 | B | Strong composition with good analytical insight. Minor gaps. |
| 70–79 | C | Competent but doesn't yet integrate constraints into a unified creative vision. |
| 60–69 | D | Technical skills present but conceptual understanding underdeveloped. |
| Below 60 | F | Not yet operating at the advanced level. |

### Advanced Feedback Templates

**For high-scoring students (90+):**
> "This is mature work. You've internalized constraint theory to the point where it's a creative instinct, not a checklist. Your analysis of [specific aspect] was particularly insightful. [Extension: consider submitting this to / performing at / developing into a larger work]."

**For middle-scoring students (70–89):**
> "Strong craftsmanship and growing conceptual depth. The gap between your compositional skill and your analytical articulation is [narrow / noticeable]. To reach the highest level, focus on [specific criterion]. The best constraint composers can explain *why* their constraints produce specific effects — work on making that connection explicit."

## Portfolio Assessment Rubric

For end-of-curriculum evaluation of the complete student portfolio.

| # | Component | Weight | Description |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | **Worksheets (1–5)** | 20% | Completion and quality of all five worksheets |
| 2 | **Composition 1 (Tightly Constrained)** | 25% | Evaluated using the appropriate level rubric above |
| 3 | **Composition 2 (Loosely Constrained / Free)** | 25% | Same rubric; should show contrast with Composition 1 |
| 4 | **Reflection Essay** | 15% | 1–2 paragraphs on how constraints affected their creative process |
| 5 | **Peer Review** | 15% | Quality of feedback given to a classmate (usefulness, specificity, constraint awareness) |

### Portfolio Grade Calculation

```
Portfolio Score = (Worksheets × 0.20) + (Comp1 × 0.25) + (Comp2 × 0.25) + (Reflection × 0.15) + (PeerReview × 0.15)
```

## Quick Grading Reference

### Red Flags (Investigate Further)

- Student always uses the exact same pitches in the same order → may not be engaging with the constraint, just memorizing
- Composition is much shorter than required → may be struggling with the creative process, not the constraints
- Reflection is copied from the glossary → conceptual understanding not yet developed
- No constraints are broken, but the composition is just a scale → playing it safe; needs encouragement to explore

### Green Flags (Strengths to Build On)

- Student breaks a constraint *intentionally* and can explain why → advanced thinking
- Composition has a clear emotional character (sad, triumphant, mysterious) → musical maturity
- Student generates their own constraints → creative independence
- Student helps peers with their compositions → deep understanding

Each generated exercise includes its own `scoring_rubric` with point breakdowns, making it easy to create consistent grading criteria across sections or semesters.

### Mapping Exercise Rubrics to These Assessment Rubrics

The exercise generator's rubrics are *specific* (e.g., "consonance: 25, lattice_snap: 25"). These assessment rubrics are *general*. For graded work using generated exercises:

1. Use the exercise's specific rubric for the compliance/technical portion (criteria 1–2 of the appropriate level)
2. Use the general rubric for musicality, creativity, and analysis (criteria 3–6)
3. Weight accordingly (beginner: compliance-heavy; advanced: analysis-heavy)
