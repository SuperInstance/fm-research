# EXPERIMENT 200: THE BICENTENNIAL

## The Question
After 199 experiments, what is the ONE question that remains?

Not "what is the sweet spot?" — we know (ρ≈18).
Not "does the cliff exist?" — we know (it's the Hopf bifurcation).
Not "is the system a monoculture?" — we know (yes, universally).

The question is: **Does the attractor surprise us?**

After 199 experiments designed by us, parameter-sweeped by us, metricated
by us — let the system design its OWN experiment. 

## The Design
Run 10,000 random parameter combinations (ρ, σ, β) for the Lorenz system.
For EACH combination, compute ALL metrics.
Find the combination that MAXIMIZES something we've never looked for:
the product of ALL metrics simultaneously (CE × Cons × Fisher × Novelty).

The system will tell us where the true optimum is — not by our metrics,
but by the conjunction of ALL metrics.

Then: listen to it. Generate the audio. The 200th experiment produces
the DEFINITIVE melody of the 200-experiment session.

## The Hypothesis
The 200th experiment will produce a melody that is:
1. NOT at ρ=18 (our assumed sweet spot)
2. NOT at ρ=47 (the old peak)
3. At some unexpected combination that no single metric would find
4. The attractor's own choice, not ours
